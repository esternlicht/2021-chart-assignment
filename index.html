Landlord Goes on Pandemic Purchase Spree in Brooklyn

Since March 2020, landlord Greenbrook Partners purchased 79 residential properties for a total of $285.3 million.

By Emily Sternlicht

Nov. 18, 2021

70 Prospect Park West in Park Slope, Brooklyn. Credit: Connect Media

Landlord Greenbrook Partners has been on a buying spree since the pandemic began, spending a total of $285.3 million on 79 buildings in Brooklyn since March 2020, according to property records.

According to an April analysis by Bloomberg, which cites data from the U.S. Postal Service and U.S. Census, 20,000 people moved from Manhattan to Brooklyn between March 2020 and February 2021, making it the most popular relocation destination for Manhattanites during the pandemic.

“Neighborhoods like Park Slope, which are full of amenities, are particularly desirable,” said Professor John Mollenkopf, director of the Center for Urban Research at the City University of New York.

Mollenkopf said that housing production in New York has not kept up with demand.

“Housing is expensive and difficult to develop,” he said, “but owners who can get properties at good prices, and who are willing and able to realize their market potential by ousting tenants, can make a lot of money.”

Greenbrook Partners is funded by London-based equity firm NW1, with a $100 million investment from the Texas School Board of Education’s pension fund. According to the NW1 website, following the December 2020 investment from the Texas Board of Education, NW1 allocated $25 million of that capital to its “multifamily strategy” in Brooklyn neighborhoods, predominantly in Park Slope.
